SIGNAL CLEVELAND

How a body camera is supposed to be used on every shift

Cleveland's policy sets six steps for each tour of duty. The middle three repeat at every public contact. Nearly every disciplinary case traces back to a single broken step.

1
START OF SHIFT
Inspect & mount the camera

Officers must inspect the camera for damage, then mount it upright on their upper chest so it captures their field of view.

2
DURING THE TOUR · repeats at every public contact
Buffering mode

In buffering mode, the cameras continuously loop thirty seconds of video with no audio. Officers leave the camera in buffering mode for the duration of their tour of duty, so the WCS can be easily placed into event mode when required.

3
Switch to event mode

Before any call or public contact, double-press the button. A tone and blinking red light confirm audio and video are recording. Thirty seconds of silent pre-event video is captured from buffering mode. When practical and safe, they should tell citizens the camera is recording.

4
Record until the contact ends

Keep recording until the contact concludes or a supervisor orders a stop. Returning to buffering early requires a spoken reason, recorded on camera.

↻ Returns to buffering, then repeats at the next contact
5
Tag and categorize

While touring, officers categorize captured footage using an online application. The Axon View app allows officers to stream footage directly to their personal phones, so they can view and tag it.

According to Axon, footage is never stored locally and the app cannot be used to alter or delete videos. Cleveland’s policy specifies officers cannot use the video for anything other than official purposes or transfer it anywhere except Evidence.com.

6
END OF SHIFT
Dock the camera and upload to Evidence.com

Place it in the docking station, which uploads the captured footage and charges the camera at the same time. Footage is encrypted and stored in the cloud, accessible to personnel based on their security clearance.
